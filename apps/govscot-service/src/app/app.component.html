Registration Deadline Warning

You must register for Corporation Tax within 3 months of starting business. You could be fined if you do not register in time.

Payment Deadline Warning

Corporation Tax is due 9 months and 1 day after the end of your accounting period. Late payment results in interest charges.

Legal Requirement Warning

It's a legal requirement to keep proper business records for at least 6 years. You could face penalties if you don't.

Basic Accordion Example

Corporation Tax is charged on the 'chargeable profits' of limited companies, members' clubs, societies, associations, co-operatives and other unincorporated bodies.

The rate of Corporation Tax depends on the amount of profit your company makes and when your accounting period ends.

You must pay Corporation Tax if your company or organisation is based in the UK or has an office or branch here.

  • limited companies
  • foreign companies with a UK branch or office
  • clubs, societies, associations and other unincorporated bodies
  • co-operatives
Check if you need to register

You must tell HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) when you start doing business as a limited company - this is usually when you:

  • sell goods or services
  • buy business stock or equipment
  • take on employees
  • rent or buy business premises

You have 3 months from when you start doing business to tell HMRC. You could be fined if you do not register in time.

You can register your company for Corporation Tax online using the Government Gateway.

What you'll need

  • your contact details
  • details of your business activities

Registration usually takes up to 10 working days, but it can take longer during busy periods.

Make sure you're aware of these important Corporation Tax deadlines:

  • Registration: Within 3 months of starting business
  • Filing return: 12 months after the end of your accounting period
  • Paying tax: 9 months and 1 day after the end of your accounting period
